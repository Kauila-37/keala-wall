## Copy — paste as-is

Two parcels of Big Island land can look identical from the road and be worth completely different money. The reasons are never visible from the road.

Before you buy vacant land here, get answers to three questions:

**1. What's under the ground?** The USGS maps every acre of this island into lava flow hazard zones 1 through 9. That number affects the price, whether you can insure it, and sometimes whether a lender will touch it.

**2. Where does the water come from — and where does it go?** Everyone asks about catchment. Almost nobody asks about the cesspool. There are roughly 48,600 on Hawaiʻi Island, and state law requires every one to be converted by January 1, 2050 — typically $30,000 to $50,000 per property.

**3. What can you actually build?** Agricultural zoning carries minimum lot sizes written right into the designation. A-1a means one acre. A-20a means twenty. It decides whether you can ever subdivide.

Answer those three and you've avoided most of the expensive surprises.
