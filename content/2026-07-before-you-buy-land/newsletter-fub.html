Three questions to ask before you buy Big Island land

Aloha,

Most of the buyers I meet on vacant land are coming from somewhere the questions are simpler. A lot is a lot. Water comes from the street. The rock beneath you is just rock.

Here, two parcels can look identical from the road and be worth wildly different money — or cost six figures apart to make livable. It almost always comes down to three things: what’s under the ground, where the water comes from, and what the county will let you build.

The one that surprises people most is the second one — and not the half you’d expect. Everybody asks about catchment. Almost nobody asks about the cesspool. There are roughly 48,600 of them on this island, and under Act 125 every single one has to be converted by January 1, 2050. That’s a $30,000–$50,000 line item, and it belongs in your budget today, not in 2049.

I wrote up all three questions, with the sources behind each one, so you can walk a parcel with your eyes open.
