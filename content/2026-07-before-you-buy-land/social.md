## LinkedIn

Lead with the correction — it's the most credible, most shareable thing in the post.

> If someone is selling you a solar package on Hawaiʻi Island right now on the strength of "the 30% federal tax credit," ask them to show you the statute.
>
> The residential credit under Section 25D was **eliminated** — not phased down — for systems installed on or after January 1, 2026. Much of the internet hasn't caught up, and buyers are still building it into their numbers.
>
> It's the kind of thing that makes me careful about the other figures too. Hawaiʻi has the highest residential electricity rates in the country at roughly 42¢/kWh, which is exactly why so much of our land gets developed off-grid — so the math on that credit was load-bearing for a lot of people's plans.
>
> I put together the three questions every Big Island land buyer should answer before they get attached to a parcel: what's under the ground (USGS lava hazard zones), where the water comes from and where it goes (the 2050 cesspool deadline), and what the zoning actually permits.
