## The one idea

A parcel here can look identical to the one next to it and be worth a fraction of the price — and
the difference is never visible from the road. Rock, water, rules.

## Structure

1. **What is the ground made of?** — USGS lava flow hazard zones 1–9. Drives price, insurability,
   financing. 2018 Kīlauea eruption destroyed 716 homes. Get the zone before you get attached.
2. **Where does the water come from — and go?** — County water vs. catchment. Then the expensive
   half nobody asks about: cesspools. ~83,000 statewide, ~48,600 on this island, all must convert
   by Jan 1 2050 under Act 125. $30k–$50k per property.
3. **What can you actually build?** — Ag zoning minimum lot sizes are baked into the designation:
   A-1a = 1 acre, A-20a = 20, A-200a = 200. Determines subdivision and dwelling rights.
   Plus: Hawaiʻi has the highest residential electricity rates in the US (~42¢/kWh, 2026), which is
   why so much land is developed off-grid — and the 30% federal residential solar credit (25D)
   **ended for systems installed on/after Jan 1 2026.**

## Closing

Every painful land transaction traces to one of those three going unasked until it was too late to
matter. The job isn't talking anyone into a parcel — it's making sure that when you fall in love
with one, you already know what it is.
