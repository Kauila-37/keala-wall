### Volcanic / lava zones (USGS)
- [ ] USGS lava hazard zones **1 (highest) – 9 (lowest)**; zone drives insurance/financing/value
- [ ] Zone assignments per area (Puna 1–4, Kona 5–6, Waimea 7–8, Kohala **9**)

### Water / catchment / wells
- [ ] **30% of Big Island residents** rely entirely on catchment (land page)
- [ ] Catchment specs: 10,000–30,000 gal tanks; 50–100 gal/person/day
- [ ] Well drilling **$30K–$80K**; DLNR CWRM approval; **6–18 mo**

### Agricultural zoning (Hawaiʻi County Code, Chapter 25) — HIGH PRIORITY
- [ ] Min lot sizes: **FA-2a = 2 ac, A-1a = 1 ac, A-20a = 20 ac, A-40 = 40 ac, A-200a = 200 ac**
- [ ] Ag dwelling must be "farm dwelling" incidental to ag use
- [ ] Ag tax exemption requires demonstrated active farming

### Energy / cost of living
- [ ] Hawaiʻi **highest electricity rates in US**; **$0.38–$0.45/kWh** vs ~$0.16 national (US EIA)
- [ ] Groceries **30–50% more** than mainland; cost of living **~40–53% higher**
- [ ] Hawaiʻi **state income tax up to 11%**
- [ ] Vehicle registration/safety inspection **$17–$25**; register within 30 days; license within 90 days

### Building / permits
- [ ] County building permit required for structures **>200 sq ft** or with plumbing/electrical/mechanical
- [ ] Conservation District builds need **BLNR** approval; **12–24 mo**
- [ ] Perc test by licensed engineer required before septic/building permit

## Notes on claim types
- **Hard facts** (geography, statute, USGS zones, EIA rates): cite primary source, safe to state definitively.
- **Prices / rents / medians:** inherently time-sensitive → attribute + date them ("as of 2026") or pull live, never state as timeless fact.
- **Superlatives / "best"** ("best schools," "one of the largest"): either source to a ranking or reframe as opinion.
- **Anything about tax credits, assistance programs, deadlines:** re-verify at publish time — these change.
