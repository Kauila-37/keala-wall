Sources & Methodology

The facts behind this site.

Real estate advice is only as good as the facts under it. Every specific claim on this site — lava zones, zoning, water, the cesspool deadline, energy costs — is checked against a credible, primary source, mostly government agencies. Those references are listed here. Time-sensitive figures are dated and re-checked periodically.

Last reviewed: July 2026. Prices, rates, and programs change — always confirm specifics for your situation before relying on them.

Volcanic & lava hazard

USGS lava hazard zones 1–9; the 2018 Kīlauea eruption destroyed 716 homes in lower Puna. U.S. Geological Survey, Hawaiian Volcano Observatory — 2018 eruption on a global scale · Lava Flow Hazard Zones

Wastewater & the 2050 cesspool deadline

Act 125 (2017) requires all ~83,000 cesspools statewide (~48,600 on Hawaiʻi Island) to be converted by January 1, 2050; conversion typically costs $30,000–$50,000. U.S. EPA — Cesspools in Hawaii · Hawaiʻi Dept. of Health — Cesspool Conversion

Agricultural zoning

Minimum lot sizes across agricultural districts — A-1a (1 acre), A-20a (20 acres), A-200a (200 acres) — under Hawaiʻi County Code Chapter 25. Hawaiʻi County Planning Dept. — Zoning & Land Use

Water systems

County water availability; well permitting through the state. Hawaiʻi County Dept. of Water Supply — DWS · Hawaiʻi DLNR Commission on Water Resource Management — CWRM

Energy costs & solar

Hawaiʻi has the highest residential electricity rates in the U.S. (~42¢/kWh, 2026). U.S. Energy Information Administration — Hawaii Electricity Profile
The 30% federal residential solar tax credit ended for systems installed on or after January 1, 2026. EnergySage — End of the residential solar credit · SEIA — Clean energy provisions

Waimea & Parker Ranch

Parker Ranch, founded 1847, spans roughly 130,000–150,000 acres and is among the largest cattle ranches in the U.S. Parker Ranch — Our Story
